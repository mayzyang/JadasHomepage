About Me

My name is May Yang and I'm a junior at the University of Michigan currently pursuing a major in Computer Science and a minor in Entrepreneurship. Ever since I was young, I've always had ambitious and creative ideas, but going beyond just daydreaming, I've loved transforming those ideas into real creations. In the summer of 2016, I was introduced to the powerful and exciting world of technology through Girls Who Code. I realized I could use Computer Science to satisfy my love of both collaboration and invention.

At the University of Michigan, I enjoy taking technical courses such as Algorithms and Data Structures, as well as liberal arts courses like Future Humanity and Women Studies. Since I've always been interested by the impact technology has on society and the associated ethics, I hope to contribute my intersectional interests in an environment where people and tangible results are prioritized.

When I'm not coding, I love skiing, hiking with my dad, road trips, browsing Amazon Prime, and watching corgi videos!

Noteable Projects:

My Choice

- My Choice is an interactive "Choose Your Own Adventure" web game that allows users to make decisions in the daily life of a female student in the US. Based on their choices, users will gain insight into the micro-aggressions and stereotypes girls face daily as a result of their personal decisions.

- Future updates will include a map API that allows users to experience a day in the life of a girl from other countries, in order to foster understanding for intersectional feminism.

- Used HTML, CSS and JavaScript to implement popups, buttons, nav-bars and other interactive features.
